# Sensor Fusion Implementation Reflection

The complementary filter implementation successfully combines the high-frequency responsiveness of the accelerometer with the low-frequency stability of the camera tracking to predict the phone's position. 

When testing with different filter weights (`alpha`), the trade-offs of the two sensors become clear. When `alpha` is set close to **1.0**, the filter heavily trusts the camera. The predicted position (green dot) stays perfectly glued to the camera position (red dot), offering high absolute accuracy without any drift, but it inherits the visual latency and lower framerate of the webcam. Conversely, when `alpha` is set close to **0.0**, the filter heavily trusts the accelerometer. The green dot reacts instantly to the physical movement of the phone, making the tracking feel incredibly smooth and responsive. However, due to the double-integration of the noisy accelerometer data, the calculated velocity quickly accumulates error, causing the green dot to "drift" away from the phone's actual physical position (requiring frequent resets). A balanced `alpha` (e.g., around **0.2 - 0.5**) provides the best of both worlds: the prediction moves instantly when the phone is jerked thanks to the accelerometer, while the camera data slowly pulls it back to the absolute center over time, completely eliminating long-term drift.
